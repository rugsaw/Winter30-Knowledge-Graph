ANNUAL PERFORMANCE OVERVIEW

ABC Industries Limited ("ABC" or "the Company") is a diversified conglomerate headquartered in India with operations spanning energy, retail, telecommunications, chemicals, and logistics. During FY 2024–25, ABC Industries reported consolidated revenues of INR 9.84 trillion, representing a year-on-year growth of 11.6% compared to INR 8.82 trillion in FY 2023–24.
The consolidated EBITDA for the year stood at INR 1.56 trillion, while net profit attributable to shareholders increased to INR 824 billion. The Company continued to focus on capital efficiency, operational resilience, and digital transformation across all business segments.

SEGMENT-WISE PERFORMANCE

Energy & Petrochemicals Segment

The Energy & Petrochemicals segment, operated primarily through ABC Energy Limited, contributed INR 5.21 trillion to consolidated revenues, accounting for approximately 53% of total revenues.
The global ethylene market experienced moderate growth of 2.8% in calendar year 2024, reaching a total demand of approximately 185 million metric tonnes. Meanwhile, the global polymer market grew by 3.2% to 254 million metric tonnes during the same period.
ABC Energy Limited operates a refinery with a capacity of 1.4 million barrels per day, making it one of the largest single-location refining complexes globally. During FY 2024–25, the refinery operated at a utilization rate of 108%.
Crude oil prices remained volatile, averaging USD 82 per barrel, compared to USD 89 per barrel in the previous fiscal year. The Company's gross refining margin averaged USD 9.7 per barrel, reflecting improved feedstock optimization and higher distillate yields.


Retail Segment

ABC Retail Ventures Limited, a wholly owned subsidiary of ABC Industries, operates more than 18,400 retail stores across India under multiple formats, including grocery, fashion, electronics, and lifestyle.
Retail revenues increased by 17.4% year-on-year to INR 3.12 trillion in FY 2024–25. The digital commerce platform, ABCMart, recorded gross merchandise value of INR 486 billion, growing at 24% compared to the previous year.
During the year, ABC Retail Ventures acquired a 54% controlling stake in Hamleys India Private Limited, expanding its footprint in the premium toy retail segment. The acquisition was valued at INR 7.2 billion and was funded through internal accruals.

Telecommunications Segment

ABC Communications Limited reported revenues of INR 1.18 trillion, an increase of 9.3% year-on-year. The average revenue per user (ARPU) improved to INR 193 per month from INR 178 in FY 2023–24.
As of March 31, 2025, the Company served over 472 million subscribers, making it the largest telecom operator in India by subscriber base. Data traffic per user increased to 29.1 GB per month, driven by increased adoption of 5G services.
Capital expenditure in the telecommunications segment amounted to INR 362 billion, primarily for network expansion and spectrum-related investments.


INVESTMENTS AND SUBSIDIARIES

ABC Industries holds strategic investments in several entities:

• ABC Logistics Limited, which operates over 1,200 warehouses across India and reported revenues of INR 142 billion.

• ABC New Energy Solutions, a renewable energy subsidiary focused on solar and green hydrogen projects, with an installed capacity of 6.4 GW.

• ABC Financial Services Limited, a non-banking financial company regulated by the Reserve Bank of India, reported assets under management of INR 3.8 trillion.

The Company increased its stake in ABC New Energy Solutions from 51% to 67% during the year through an equity infusion of INR 48 billion.


GEOGRAPHIC DISTRIBUTION

India continued to be the primary market, contributing 71% of consolidated revenues. International operations, primarily in Asia, Europe, and North America, accounted for the remaining 29%.
Revenues from the Asia-Pacific region grew by 14%, while European revenues declined by 3.6% due to macroeconomic headwinds and reduced consumer demand.


RISK FACTORS

The Company is exposed to multiple risks, including:

• Volatility in crude oil and natural gas prices

• Foreign exchange fluctuations, particularly USD-INR movements

• Regulatory changes in telecommunications tariffs

• Supply chain disruptions impacting logistics and retail operations

• Climate-related risks affecting energy and agricultural supply chains

Cybersecurity threats remain a key concern, especially for digital platforms and telecom infrastructure.


CAPITAL STRUCTURE AND LIQUIDITY

As of March 31, 2025, ABC Industries reported gross debt of INR 3.41 trillion, with a net debt-to-EBITDA ratio of 1.9x. Cash and cash equivalents stood at INR 1.27 trillion.
The Company raised INR 210 billion through long-term bonds during the year, while repaying INR 184 billion of existing borrowings.


OUTLOOK AND FORWARD-LOOKING STATEMENTS

Looking ahead, the Company expects domestic polymer demand to grow at a compound annual growth rate of 6–7% over the next three years. Retail consumption is expected to remain resilient, supported by urban demand and digital penetration.
Planned capital expenditure for FY 2025–26 is estimated at INR 1.05 trillion, with a significant portion allocated to renewable energy and digital infrastructure projects.
Management remains cautiously optimistic, while acknowledging ongoing global economic uncertainties and geopolitical risks.
